Random Number Formula Explained

Understanding Math.floor(Math.random() * (max - min + 1) + min)

What does this formula do?

This formula generates a random integer between a minimum value (min) and a maximum value (max), inclusive.

In your code, min = 10 and max = 20, so it generates random numbers between 10 and 20.

Breaking down the formula step by step:

1. Math.random()

Generates a random decimal number between 0 (inclusive) and 1 (exclusive).

Example: 0.354, 0.876, 0.123, etc.

2. (max - min + 1)

Calculates the range of possible values.

For min=10 and max=20: (20 - 10 + 1) = 11

This means we have 11 possible integers (10, 11, 12, ..., 20).

3. Math.random() * (max - min + 1)

Multiplies the random decimal by the range.

Example: 0.354 * 11 = 3.894

This gives us a number between 0 (inclusive) and 11 (exclusive).

4. Math.floor()

Rounds the number down to the nearest integer.

Example: Math.floor(3.894) = 3

This gives us an integer between 0 and 10 (inclusive).

5. + min

Adds the minimum value to shift the range.

Example: 3 + 10 = 13

This shifts our range from 0-10 to 10-20.
